DOE Security Force

The Department of Energy Security Force (DOE SF) is one of the primary security organizations within the Black Mesa Research Facility. Operating under the U.S. Department of Energy, this unit is tasked with securing high-risk research zones and ensuring containment of experimental threats.

Primary Roles

• Guarding top-secret research sectors and controlling access
• Securing hazardous material zones
• First-response operations during anomalies and emergencies
• Executive protection for VIPs and senior personnel
• Coordinated missions with other Black Mesa security teams

Equipment & Tactical Capability

DOE Security personnel are equipped with advanced gear, including tactical armor, night-vision systems, biometric monitoring (ELM), and encrypted communications. Many operations are jointly conducted with the Black Mesa Special Security Force (SSF) and the First Response Tactical Operations Unit (FRTOU).

Training & Veteran Status

Recruits are typically drawn from military or high-security backgrounds and undergo continuous high-intensity tactical training.
Veterans who serve in DOE Security for extended periods may transition to the Black Mesa Security Division (BMSD), often earning distinction with unique collar pins that signify their service years.

Secrecy & External Operations

All DOE Security activities are strictly classified. When operating beyond the facility, agents may be disguised as "DOE Technical Inspection Teams" or "Energy Safety Delegates" for covert assignments.
